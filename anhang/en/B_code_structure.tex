This appendix documents the physical structure of the implementation as it exists in the
source tree. It complements the conceptual description in the implementation chapter: where
that chapter explains \emph{why} the framework is decomposed into interchangeable axes behind
one ABI-stable interface, this appendix records \emph{where} each concept lives in the code.
The listing is a faithful cartography of the real directories and headers; identifiers are
given verbatim so that a reader can navigate the repositories directly.

\section{Three-repository topology}\label{asec:code-topology}
The implementation is split across three Git repositories consumed as parallel submodules. The
super-repository (\texttt{Diplomarbeit/Code}) is the \emph{application layer} that defines
\emph{what} is measured; the cache engine is the reusable \emph{framework} that defines
\emph{how}; and PRT-ART is the \emph{probe} (the thesis' own algorithm) under test.
Table~\ref{tab:code:repos} summarises the three roles.

\section{The cache engine (framework)}\label{asec:code-ce}
The cache engine lives under \texttt{Code/external/comdare-cache-engine}. Its core is the
library tree \texttt{libs/}; around it sit \texttt{apps/} (executables), \texttt{tools/} (build
and provisioning helpers), \texttt{tests/}, \texttt{ext/} (vendored third-party algorithm code),
\texttt{cmake/} and \texttt{adapters/}.

\subsection{Three-layer libraries}\label{asec:code-libs}

The remainder of this section drills into \texttt{libs/cache\_engine}, which carries the thesis'
core contribution.

\subsection{The axis roof and the nineteen organ axes}\label{asec:code-axes}
All axis families share one semantics-free \emph{roof}, \texttt{topics/axis.hpp}: a marker
concept \texttt{Axis<Derived>} (an empty CRTP base with no vtable) plus a family discriminator
\texttt{AxisKind} (\texttt{organ}, \texttt{system\_measurement}, \texttt{system\_config}). The
roof carries only identity; each family keeps its own semantics in its own sub-root so that organ
and system axes are never mixed. The \texttt{topics/} directory groups the family roots
(\texttt{allocator}, \texttt{prefetch}, \texttt{memory\_layout}, \texttt{nodes},
\texttt{traversal}, \texttt{telemetry}, \texttt{serialization}, \texttt{concurrency},
\texttt{value\_handle}, \texttt{hardware}, \texttt{io}, \texttt{filter}, \texttt{queuing},
\texttt{migration}, \texttt{sequence}, \texttt{view}, \texttt{search\_engine}), each with a
\texttt{topics::AxisBase} CRTP base guarded by concepts.

The concrete strategies live under \texttt{axes/}, grouped into family directories:
\texttt{index\_organization}, \texttt{path\_compression}, \texttt{lookup},
\texttt{cache\_traversal}, \texttt{mapping}, \texttt{node}, \texttt{layout}, \texttt{alloc},
\texttt{prefetch\_axis}, \texttt{concurrency\_axis}, \texttt{serialization\_axis},
\texttt{telemetry\_axis}, \texttt{value\_handle\_axis}, \texttt{simd}, \texttt{io\_dispatch},
\texttt{migration\_policy}, \texttt{filter\_axis} and \texttt{cacheline}. Each family provides a
strategy base, a concept, an observable wrapper, a registry and several concrete strategies (for
example the search-algorithm family alone reconstructs ART, SuRF, Wormhole, HOT, B-tree,
Eytzinger, $k$-ary, interpolation, hash and linear variants).

A composed \emph{living being} is identified by a \texttt{binary\_id} that permutes nineteen
organ-axis slots. Their canonical order is fixed by \texttt{kCompositionAxisNames} in
\texttt{builder/experiment\_tree/axis\_path\_serialization.hpp} and reproduced in
Table~\ref{tab:code:axes}.

The allocator axis (T6, \texttt{axes/alloc}) illustrates the design: every allocator wrapper is
the algorithm choice itself, realised as a compile-time CRTP strategy with a concept guard rather
than a vtable. A single central \texttt{Boost.MP11} type list \texttt{AllVendors} (around two dozen
vendor wrappers, from \texttt{std\_malloc} and the polymorphic-resource variants to mimalloc,
jemalloc, tcmalloc, snmalloc, rpmalloc and further research allocators) is filtered at compile time
to \texttt{EnabledVendors}; each generated per-permutation translation unit bakes in exactly one choice.

\subsection{The system-axis layer}\label{asec:code-sysaxes}
Above the organ axes sits a layer of \emph{system axes} under
\texttt{include/cache\_engine/measurement}. They share the axis roof through two sibling roots:
\texttt{SystemAxis} (measurement sources, always present, golden-neutral) and
\texttt{CebSystemAxis} (configuration axes that steer how the subordinate tier binaries are built
and measured). The configuration system axes never permute the \texttt{binary\_id} and never appear
in \texttt{kCompositionAxisNames}. The layer is materialised in
\texttt{scheduling\_system\_axis.hpp}, \texttt{hardware\_isa\_system\_axis.hpp},
\texttt{load\_framework\_system\_axis.hpp}, \texttt{extension\_hardware\_system\_axis.hpp} and
their common roots \texttt{system\_axis.hpp} / \texttt{ceb\_system\_axis.hpp}.

\subsection{Anatomy: genus, living being, organs}\label{asec:code-anatomy}
The anatomy model under \texttt{anatomy/} is the counterpart to the search-API layers. A
\emph{genus} (\texttt{AnatomyGattung}) fixes the outer hull of a family (search algorithm,
sequential container, set, graph, view); a \emph{living being} is one composed configuration; the
\emph{organs} are its axis strategies. The directory carries a permutation engine and an anatomy
per genus (\texttt{search\_algorithm\_anatomy.hpp}, \texttt{sequence\_anatomy.hpp},
\texttt{set\_anatomy.hpp}, \texttt{view\_anatomy.hpp}), the check dock (\texttt{pruef\_dock.hpp})
and the ABI-stable tier interfaces: the consolidated observer snapshot
(\texttt{observable\_tier.hpp}), the flat resource-control POD
(\texttt{resource\_controllable\_tier.hpp}) and the drive/rollback/scan sub-interfaces. Each genus
provides its own ABI adapter (\texttt{*\_abi\_adapter.hpp}).

\subsection{Builder and code generation}\label{asec:code-builder}
The \texttt{builder/} subtree turns a registry description into concrete, statically compiled
per-permutation binaries. Its central pieces are \texttt{experiment\_tree/} (the axis-level
reflection \texttt{registry\_to\_axis\_levels.hpp}, path serialisation, and the lazy composition
iterator), \texttt{build\_orchestrator/} (the compile-function factory that emits the response
files for the shared objects), \texttt{codegen/} and \texttt{permutation\_codegen\_tool/} (the
per-permutation translation-unit generation), \texttt{pruef\_dock/} (the check-dock driver),
\texttt{anatomy\_module\_loader/} (the \texttt{dlopen}-based module loader), and the measurement
machinery (\texttt{measurement/}, \texttt{measurement\_matrix/},
\texttt{in\_memory\_measurement\_buffer/}, the PMC sources and the provenance manifest).

\subsection{Profile facade}\label{asec:code-facade}
The \texttt{profile\_facade/} directory is the entry point that a caller uses to run an
experiment profile. It holds the run facade (\texttt{profile\_run\_facade.\{hpp,cpp\}}), the
experiment-run and profile-run entries, the profile validator and the two catalogues
(\texttt{source\_catalog.hpp}, \texttt{sota\_catalog.hpp}) that define the golden pilot set and
the reconstructed state-of-the-art points.

\subsection{Concepts, ABI and public API}\label{asec:code-concepts}
The public headers live under \texttt{include/cache\_engine}. The \texttt{concepts/} directory
holds the interface concepts (observer, sub-engine, telemetry strategy, concurrency manager,
scheduling strategy and the deprecated runtime-vtable siblings), the \texttt{abi/} directory holds
the module ABI (\texttt{anatomy\_module\_abi\_v1\_decl.hpp} with the ABI major, the module loader
declaration and the configuration-permutation record), \texttt{measurement/} holds the system
axes and the measurement record, and \texttt{api/} holds the public \texttt{i\_cache\_engine.hpp}.

\subsection{Applications and tools}\label{asec:code-apps}
The \texttt{apps/} directory contains the executables: the cache-engine builder, the permutation
and catalog code-generation tools, the anatomy code-generation tool, the permutation runner, the
\texttt{f15\_compare} comparison front-end and the \texttt{is\_original\_validator} (which produces
the reproducibility locks). The \texttt{tools/} directory contains the supporting toolchain: the
axis-registry generator, the ABI-stability test, the LaTeX toolchain, the best-binary selector,
compiler provisioning and the YCSB workload command-line interface.

\section{PRT-ART (the probe)}\label{asec:code-prtart}
PRT-ART lives under \texttt{Code/external/comdare-prt-art}; its source root is \texttt{prt\_art/}.
It is the thesis' own probe algorithm, a Probst-Redirect-Tree/ART hybrid with its own
building-block layers, and it is consumed by the engine as one module rather than being wired in
hard. The source root groups \texttt{algorithm\_profiles/} (its building-block profiles),
\texttt{codegen/}, \texttt{permutations\_codegen/} and \texttt{registry\_gen/} (its own
generation path), \texttt{legacy\_reimpl/} (faithful re-implementations), \texttt{src/},
\texttt{include/} and \texttt{tests/}.

\section{Application layer (\texttt{Diplomarbeit/Code})}\label{asec:code-super}
The super-repository drives the measurements and turns the binary measurement records into the
LaTeX artefacts of this thesis. Its result pipeline is realised as nine numbered stages
(Table~\ref{tab:code:pipeline}); stage~02 is the measurement driver that calls the engine and the
probe. Alongside the stages sit \texttt{experiment\_config/} (the three mandatory experiment
configurations \texttt{config\_a\_prt\_art\_vs\_sota.xml}, \texttt{config\_b\_cache\_engine\_perms.xml},
\texttt{config\_c\_merge\_alt\_neu.xml} plus \texttt{messreihen.xml}), \texttt{tests/},
\texttt{cmake/} and \texttt{tools/}. The complete measurement series produced by this pipeline is
reproduced in Appendix~\ref{app:measurements}.
